# ADR-002: SR 11-7 Alignment as Core Governance Framework

**Status:** Accepted
**Date:** 2025-03-01
**Authors:** Model Risk Management Team, Platform Engineering

---

## Context

Enterprise AI Guardian must be accepted by bank regulators (Fed Reserve, OCC) as a legitimate model risk management system. The primary regulatory guidance for model risk management in US banks is **SR 11-7: Guidance on Model Risk Management** (Federal Reserve, April 2011), supplemented by OCC Bulletin 2011-12.

The platform must demonstrate compliance with SR 11-7 to be credible in a pitch to a bank's CRO and model risk function.

---

## Decision

SR 11-7 is adopted as the **primary structural framework** for Enterprise AI Guardian's governance model. All other regulatory frameworks (ECOA, EU AI Act, DORA, Basel III) are layered on top.

### SR 11-7 Mapping to Platform Capabilities

| SR 11-7 Requirement | Platform Implementation |
|---|---|
| Model inventory with ownership | `ai_models` table with `owner`, `department`, `category` |
| Model purpose and scope documented | `AIModel.description` + Model Card |
| Risk tiering (materiality) | `RiskAssessment` with Tier 1/2/3 classification |
| Independent validation | `LifecycleStage(Validation)` + `ComplianceControl` checklist |
| Conceptual soundness assessment | Validation control: "Conceptual soundness assessment performed" |
| Data quality documentation | `DataLineage` entity + "Data quality and representativeness validated" control |
| Ongoing performance monitoring | `PerformanceMetric` time-series with PSI, AUC, drift alerting |
| Model limitations documented | Validation controls + `RiskAssessment.notes` |
| Governance framework | Full `LifecycleStage` workflow with `ApprovalRecord` |
| Audit trail | `AuditLog` (immutable, append-only) |

### LLM Extension

SR 11-7 was written for statistical/ML models. The platform extends SR 11-7 coverage to LLMs via additional governance fields:
- `prompt_injection_risk` (security dimension)
- `hallucination_rate` (output quality dimension)
- `output_filtering` (control dimension)
- `human_in_loop_required` (oversight dimension)
- `rag_enabled` + RAG quality monitoring (data quality dimension)

---

## Consequences

**Positive:**
- Platform is immediately recognisable to MRM officers and bank examiners
- Control checklists map directly to SR 11-7 examination criteria
- Extends naturally to OCC 2011-12 (near-identical framework)

**Negative:**
- SR 11-7 language (model, validation, materiality) does not map 1:1 to LLM governance terminology — requires education for GenAI teams
- SR 11-7 predates LLMs — some controls need interpretation (e.g., "backtesting" for a chatbot)

---

## Alternatives Considered

| Alternative | Rejected Because |
|---|---|
| NIST AI RMF as primary framework | Less familiar to bank MRM teams; not regulation |
| ISO 42001 | Too new; not yet referenced by bank regulators |
| Internal proprietary framework | Wouldn't be credible without regulatory anchor |
